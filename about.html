About Us

Studio Agencies Ltd is a creative In-house incepted in 2012 and incorporated in 2014 under the founder Architect Peter Wajero, Erastus Aunga and Mugambi . Our heartful speciality is to continuously provide new and innovative Architectural, structural designs and building solutions, Interior and spatial design solutions in design, Project management and full project installations. StudioAgencies Ltd is a design and build company employing building specialists in the key areas of the Building Industry. We have a wide network serving the Kenyan market, East Africa and beyond as well.

Our Philosophy

In order to produce good Architecture, an Architect must always start with the right intentions. At studio Agencies Ltd, we believe that Architecture represents a means to give man an existential foothold. We therefore always see the conception of the work of Architecture as a concretization of life situations. To us an Architectural product is a symbol, that is a work of art, which represents life situations.

When approaching an Architectural problem we do so appreciating that the purpose of Architecture transcends functionalism, for Architecture has both physical and practical implications. Since one of the basic needs of man is to experience his life situations as meaningful, at studios Agencies Ltd we ensure that the purpose of every work of Architecture we are involved in is to keep and transmit meanings.

At Studio Agencies Ltd , every Architect subscribes to what the firm has discovered over its substantial years of practice, that there are no different kinds of Architecture, but only different situations which require different solutions in order to satisfy manâ€™s emotional and physical needs. By appreciating this, we are able to ensure our Architectural solutions satisfy the design objectives appropriate to timeless Architecture.

In any work of Architecture, that we get involved in, we strive to provide our client with a dwelling. In an existential sense, dwelling is the purpose of Architecture. For a man dwells when he can orientate himself within and identify himself with the environment. Under such circumstances he experiences the environment as meaningful. At StudioAgencies Ltd , every Architect is aware that good Architecture aims at visualizing the genius loci, or the spirit of the place and that a good Architect knows that his primary task is to create meaningful places that helps man dwell.

At StudioAgencies Ltd, we appreciate a good visual design as an important aspect of Architecture but complete Architecture is only obtainable when an Architect also produces a sound and useful building. Therefore, we do not strive to achieve good aesthetics at the expense of function and stability. At StudioAgencies, we endeavor to satisfy the fundamental principles of Architecture but we distaste coarse and falsely stylistic Architecture. Our design process instead has three major components namely; Conceptual Design, Geometric Design and Operational Design.

The Conceptual arrangement is largely a statement of intent for the guidance of Structural and Service Engineering Consultants and for use in obtaining information from many manufacturers and suppliers who will be involved in the work. The Geometric Design deals with the detailed visual inter-relationships between all parts of the building as the Operational stage develops.

The Operational design deals with solving of problems associated with individual components and their assembly and integration in the total design. The Operational stage is in essence the work done by the Architect and Specialists in the investigation of their own area of work, but within the framework of the basic concept. By approaching a design problem this way, it is easier for the design team to reach major agreements in an easier and quicker manner. This avoids major changes being introduced after a particular design stage, a situation which if allowed to occur, tends to frustrate the work of others. The smooth working of the team tends to be destroyed if one member upsets the others.
